Analysis
The business in brief

Darling's is a pure-service operation in Warren, PA — essentially all revenue, zero COGS. The model is entirely labor-driven: wages and payroll taxes run ~78–80% of revenue, making margin a function of billing rates, labor efficiency, and overhead control.

All revenue flows through a single non-taxable sales line consistent with Medicaid and waiver billing. The absence of a payer breakdown is the most significant information gap at this stage.

The headline story

The business lost money at the operating line in 2024, then swung to $216K operating income in 2025 — a $240K improvement on only $208K of additional revenue. That's a cost story: rent –$30K, car expenses –$19K, software/IT –$14.5K, insurance –$9K account for nearly the entire margin recovery.

The critical question is whether those reductions are durable post-acquisition — particularly rent, which has dropped to effectively zero, suggesting the owner may be operating out of a personally-owned or home office space.

2026 is pacing ahead: $364K in ~2.5 months implies a ~$1.74M annualized run rate at 17.9% operating margin. If the trend holds, annualized EBITDA approaches $310–320K before add-backs.

Going-forward narrative

Demand is structural: Warren County's 23.7% age 65+ is nearly 50% above the national average, with ~44,000 rural Pennsylvanians turning 65 annually through 2028. Thin documented competition (~3–6 licensed agencies) suggests strong local positioning.

The primary risk is caregiver supply, not demand. Rural, declining-population markets face a structural direct care workforce shortage — turnover costs, recruiting spend, and overtime exposure will determine whether the 2025–2026 margin expansion holds. Stress-test the P&L at 5–10% higher labor costs to establish your downside floor.
